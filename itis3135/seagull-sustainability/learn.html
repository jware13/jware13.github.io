Space Suit

Lunar dust is one of the most prevalent issues in astronaut safety. Unlike dust on Earth, lunar dust carries a strong electric charge and has sharp edges, posing as a serious risk to astronauts. As they step through the lunar surface, their suits build with charge transferred from the lunar surface. Eventually, it leads to a shock for the astronaut. One day, we can see our Simply Sea film creating a Faraday’s cage for the astronauts. The polarity, flexibility, and small pore size of our Simply Sea film could make for a durable, out-of-the-world solution to lunar dust!

Dust Shield

NASA’s Electrodynamic Dust Shield uses electric fields to repel lunar dust from surfaces such as glass and radiators. Successfully tested on the Moon, the technology clears up to 98% of dust in seconds. This innovation is crucial for protecting future lunar habitats and equipment.
